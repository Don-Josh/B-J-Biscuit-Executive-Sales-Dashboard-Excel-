# Introduction

## B&J Biscuit Executive Sales Dashboard (Excel)

This interactive Excel dashboard was designed to support data-driven decision-making for B&J Biscuit by providing a clear and insightful overview of sales performance, customer segments, and revenue drivers. Built with advanced Excel features including PivotTables, slicers, and VBA macros, it enables users to dynamically explore key metrics and discover business opportunities.

## Key Features:
### KPI Highlights:
- Quantity Sold: ****3,050,309 units****

- Total Revenue: $61.57M

- Total Profit: $26.78M

- Profit Margin: 44%

### Top Insights at a Glance:

- Most profitable brand: Shortbread

- Most profitable location: Dallas

- Highest revenue-generating customer: Robert Hernandez

- Top salesperson: Travis Doyle

- Most profitable age group: 15–29 years

### Interactive Filtering: 
Users can filter across payment types, buyer locations, and age groups for customized analysis.

### VBA Macro Integration: 
A single-click “Clear Slicer” button resets filters to default, improving user experience.

### Toggle Between View Modes: 
Users can switch between absolute values and percentage distributions for better context.

## Tools & Skills Demonstrated:
- Microsoft Excel (Advanced Modeling & Dashboards)

- Power Query for data transformation

- PivotTables & PivotCharts for multi-dimensional analysis

- VBA Macros for automation (e.g., slicer reset function)

- Data cleaning, aggregation, and visualization

- Business storytelling through visuals and insights

## Data Cleaning:
- The Data was thoroughly checked there were no duplicate values, null values and the data is consistent.

 ## Data Transformation:
- New calculated columns were created for:

- Profit (Revenue – COGS)

- Profit Margin (%)

- Age Groups (e.g., 15–29, 30–44) for demographic segmentation

- Price Range

- Customer Segments for focused analysis

Data was transformed and structured to support multi-level drill-downs and responsive slicers.

## Insights:
- Dominance of Premium Products: 90% of revenue comes from expensive products, indicating strong customer preference for high-value items.

- Young Buyers Are Key: The 15–29 age group, while not the highest in contribution, is the most profitable—suggesting lower acquisition costs or higher repeat purchases.

- Male Buyers Drive Sales: Males account for 84.4% of revenue, showing a significant demographic skew.

- Geographic Revenue Consistency: While no single city dominates, San Antonio shows slightly higher revenue (11.9%)—making it a potential location for deeper market penetration.

## Recommendations:
#### Expand Shortbread Product Line: 
- Capitalize on its profitability by launching new flavors or bundles targeted at younger age groups.

#### Loyalty Programs for Young Adults: 
- Engage the 15–29 age segment through targeted promotions and retention strategies.

#### Refocus Marketing Spend: 
- Given the gender disparity, consider campaigns targeting male buyers while exploring ways to increase engagement from other genders.

#### Target San Antonio and Dallas for Growth: 
- Invest in regional campaigns or partnerships in these high-performing cities.

#### Mobile Payment Optimization: 
- With mobile payments nearly equal in usage to other payment methods, optimizing mobile checkout could further increase conversions.
